Chef John's Yucatan-Style Grilled Pork

Description

This is not an authentic Yucatan recipe, but it's similar to dishes which could be spotted in sport bars in this region of the World. It has vibrant sour and spicy flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 oranges, juiced/li>
  • 2 lemons, juiced or more to taste
  • 2 limes, juiced, or more to taste
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 pork tenderloins, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Spices:
    • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
    • 1 teaspoon annatto powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground dried chipotle pepper
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Make seasoning. Mix orange juice, lemon juice lime juice, garlic, salt, annatto powder, chipotle powder, ground cumin, cayenne, oregano and black pepper.
  2. Cut pork tenderloins in quarters. Place in marinade prepared in 1st point. Leave in refrigerator for 4 to 6 hours
  3. Transfer pork from marinade and use paper-towels to absorb most of the moisture. Drizzle pork with vegetable oil and bit more annatto powder.
  4. Preheat an outdoor grill and lightly oil the grate
  5. Place meat pieces on a hot, medium-high heat grill. Grill pork 4 to 5 minutes on each side until brown. Transfer onto platter and allow meat to rest for about 5 minutes before serving.
